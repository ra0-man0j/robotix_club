Events & Workshops

Our Events

Our team always aspires to bring new and innovative ideas to the students of the institution.

For this we continue bringing forward competition for the students like Robo Shoot, Maze Runner, Line Follower and many more.
These Events aims on development of Robotics and Intuitive Designing of parts and bots so that the overall engineering skills of the student's grow. The club doesn't discriminate among branches or year of study, every student is given equal opportunities to participate and grow.
The following competitions are organized by our Club every year during TechFest of NIT Raipur :

  • Robo Shoot

    Soccer for robots, Score big win bigger. This competition is to show how good and swift robot of yours can play soccer and score goals. Numerous colleges participated in the event in which your robot has to score a goal against the opponent's robot. The designing of such robots required intricate and compact design while not compromising on power. It also required innovative thinking and swift running of the robot.

  • Maze Runner

    Solve it like Harry did. The game is to make a bot that can solve the maze in a given time. Its a line following bot and you're judged based on time you took. Here the use of IR sensors and related technology is very essential. The students have to keep in mind that the robot is in control and don't go off track. There's good use of coding and a lot of testing is required to get this bot working flawlessly.

  • Terrain Treader

    If your bot can go through the dirt then it can go through this. Here your bot's maneuverability and power is tested. The track is filled with obstacles and hurdles. The winner takes it all. There's a good use of high power and toughness in this not. The participants must make sure there bot functions in rigid and rough conditions without failing. The obstacle track is brutal and doesn't forgive anyone. An agile and tough bot is required for getting gold here.

  • Diabolic Dumper

    The competition requires you to design such a mechanism and shift maximum number of balls to deposit zone. Easy?? Here comes the twist in the tale. Your opponent can claim the balls you have collected. You have only two options- get dumped or become the DIABOLIC DUMPER. Not just power it's a game of speed and agility. You may not be the most powerful out of the competitors but if you're the fastest then this is the place for you to be. Stop and get lost.

  • Aqua Bot

    Make a bot that can swim its way through the water. Wired or autonomous it must clear the track underwater. The track has obstacles but the main thing is your bot must be faster than others and should go around the opponents to win the crown. Here the students have to put their brain caps on! as this bot requires a lot of calibration and modifications to make it waterproof. Since it has high chances of failure due to water leakage this bot is really a challenge for all the participants.

Our Workshops

Apart from the events Robotix Club also conducts various workshops for teaching students the basics of robotics.

These workshops focus on the overall development of the students starting from giving them the skills necessary for starting from scratch to inspiring them to come up with new and innovative ideas for doing something out of the box. These workshops include:



Artificial Intelligence


This workshop aimed at teaching Deep learning and Artificial Intelligence in robotics. It gave the students insight on self thinking and decision making algorithms. Since this plays a key role in making AI driven robots it's a very essential skill that students must have if they want to develop their self thinking robots. This was also essential and would be useful for students who are inclined towards coding and learning new algorithms. The workshop made sure that every student is capable of making a simple AI bot at the end.

Arduinobotics


Arduino is the most useful and simple tool for robotics. Easy programming and a wide variety of application gives Arduino the power that very less gadgets possess. The Arduinobotics workshop was aimed on making sure that every student that attended it leaves the Workshop with a complete knowledge of Arduino, from its capabilities to it's limits. It also provided students with Arduinos at a cheaper price and every student made their own robots on the last day of the workshop using the knowledge they gained from the two day workshop.


Drone Technology


Most exciting and in trend topic is none other than Drones. The Workshop aimed on teaching Drone Technology and Drone Applications. It also taught IR application and communication between devices. Drones are in itself very intricate and carefully assembled piece of hardware whose function always fascinates the observer. This Workshop was aimed at explaining this working of the Drones and explaining the various parameters of the same.

IC Engine


The Workshop taught basic functionality of an Internal Combustion Engine. It also talked about the details involved in a 4 Stroke Engine. An IC Engine is a very wide topic where a lot of small parameters affect it's working. These all were discussed during the workshop which contributed to the growth of knowledge which was restricted to classrooms before.

Free Workshops & Exhibitions

These were the paid workshops apart from these there were numerous Free workshops and Exhibitions aimed on inspiring and teaching students about the core knowledge of robotics and automated devices.

The free workshops included exhibitions of previously made projects from the students of the robotics club, Activities and small projects teaching how simple robots can be made easily using less resources. Also teaching basics including what parts are essential or where to get these parts etc.


Result : Overall the events organized by the robotics club has always contributed towards the growth of the grey matter of students.
